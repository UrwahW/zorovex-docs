**Task:** AI-008B

# Summary

AI-008A transformed the AI Runtime into a deterministic decision engine.

AI-008B transforms it into a true **Conversational Business Runtime**.

The AI should no longer treat every incoming message as an isolated request. Instead, every execution should understand the active conversation, customer context, workflow progress, collected information, previous AI responses, and pending questions before making a business decision.

This PR also replaces the rigid booking state machine with an adaptive requirement-driven booking engine that behaves like a professional receptionist instead of a form.

No new AI capabilities are introduced. This PR completes the Runtime architecture by adding conversational context and intelligent dialogue management.

# New Runtime Services

## ConversationContextBuilder

Responsible for assembling everything the AI needs before making decisions.

Builds:

- Recent customer messages
- Recent AI replies
- Conversation summary
- Active workflow session
- Workflow state
- Pending questions
- Collected booking information
- Customer identity
- Contact completeness
- Business context
- Thread metadata

Every execution must receive the complete conversation context.

---

## DialogueState

Tracks conversational progress independently from workflows.

Stores:

- Current topic
- Active intent
- Active goal
- Pending question
- Expected answer type
- Collected entities
- Last AI action
- Current conversation summary
- Number of misunderstandings
- Clarification attempts

DialogueState belongs to the Runtime, not individual workflows.

---

## DialoguePlanner

Determines what the AI should do next.

Possible actions include:

- Answer
- Ask Clarifying Question
- Recommend Service
- Recommend Staff
- Collect Customer Information
- Continue Workflow
- Confirm Information
- Escalate
- Close Conversation

The planner should minimize unnecessary questions while maintaining conversational flow.

---

# Booking Requirement Engine

Replace rigid booking states with dynamic requirement evaluation.

Instead of:

```
Service
↓

Staff
↓

Date
↓

Time
```

the Runtime evaluates booking requirements.

Possible requirements include:

- Service
- Category
- Preferred Staff
- Staff Recommendation
- Date
- Time
- Duration
- Customer Name
- Phone Number
- Email
- Deposit
- Payment Method
- Special Notes

Only missing requirements should be collected.

# Adaptive Booking Behaviour

Examples

---

### Returning Customer

Customer:

> Book me with Sarah tomorrow.

Known:

- Customer identity
- Phone
- Preferred stylist Sarah

AI:

> Sarah has availability tomorrow at 2:00 PM and 4:30 PM. Which time would you prefer?

No unnecessary questions.

---

### New Customer

Customer:

> I'd like balayage.

AI:

> We'd be happy to help! Do you already have a preferred stylist, or would you like us to recommend someone who specializes in balayage?

---

Customer:

> Recommend someone.

AI:

> Emma specializes in balayage and colour transformations. Sarah is also available if you'd like another option.

---

### Business Has One Staff Member

AI skips stylist questions entirely.

---

### Customer Doesn't Care

Customer:

> Anyone is fine.

AI:

> Perfect. I'll look for the earliest available appointment.

---

### Customer Changes Their Mind

Customer:

"I want balayage."

↓

"Actually maybe highlights."

DialogueState updates the selected service instead of restarting the booking.

---

### Customer Interrupts Conversation

Customer:

"I'd like a haircut."

↓

"What time do you close today?"

↓

AI answers.

↓

Returns to booking automatically.

Facebook PSID

WhatsApp Number

- New Lead

# Conversation Memory

The Runtime must understand references to previous messages.

Example

Customer:

"I'd like balayage."

↓

AI:

"What day would you like?"

↓

Customer:

"Tomorrow."

Runtime understands:

Tomorrow refers to booking date.

Not a brand-new conversation.

---

Example

Customer:

"Actually make it Friday."

The Runtime updates the booking date instead of asking what appointment the customer is referring to.

---

Example

Customer:

"No, the second option."

Runtime resolves references using recent AI replies.
